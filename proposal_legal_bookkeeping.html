⚖️ GLC Obligation: Client Accounts (Trust Accounting)

Under the Attorneys-at-Law (Accounts and Records) Act, all Jamaican attorneys are required to maintain a separate Client Account (trust account) distinct from the firm's Office Account. The General Legal Council (GLC) conducts inspections and can revoke practising certificates for non-compliance. We configure QuickBooks Online to maintain this strict separation — protecting your licence and your clients.

📋 TAJ Compliance: Registrations, GCT & Payroll Remittances

Legal services are subject to General Consumption Tax (GCT) at 15%. Law firms must file monthly GCT returns with Tax Administration Jamaica (TAJ), remit PAYE and statutory deductions (NIS, NHT, Education Tax, HEART/NSTA) monthly, and file annual income tax returns. We ensure every deadline is met and every dollar is correctly coded.

📌 Note on GCT for Law Firms

Legal services are standard-rated at 15% GCT. Disbursements recovered from clients (court fees, registration fees, etc.) may be excluded from GCT if properly documented as pass-through costs. We ensure your invoicing and accounting correctly distinguish between taxable legal fees and exempt disbursements — a frequent compliance error in Jamaican law firms.

Jamaica-Specific

What General Bookkeepers Miss

Critical Jamaican legal and tax nuances that a generalist firm typically gets wrong

1

GLC Client Account Compliance

The Attorneys-at-Law (Accounts and Records) Act mandates strict client account records. GLC inspectors can and do revoke practising certificates for accounting failures.

2

GCT on Legal Services

Legal fees are GCT-taxable at 15%. However, court filing fees and stamp duty passed through to clients are generally not — the distinction must be correctly reflected on every invoice and in QBO.

3

Jamaican Capital Allowances

Jamaica uses Initial Allowance (20%) and Annual Depreciation Allowance — not standard accounting depreciation. Tax and book values diverge and must be reconciled for corporate tax returns.

4

Four Statutory Payroll Obligations

NIS, NHT, Education Tax, and HEART/NSTA must all be computed, withheld, and remitted monthly. Missing the 14th-of-month deadline triggers penalties and interest from TAJ.

5

Contractor Withholding Tax

Payments to unregistered contractors must have 33⅓% withheld and remitted to TAJ. Many firms overlook this for freelancers, process servers, and investigators.

6

WIP & Billing Lockup

Work-in-progress lockup (unbilled time + uncollected bills) is one of the biggest cash flow risks for Jamaican law firms. We surface it monthly so partners can act.

7

Multi-Currency Exposure

Many Jamaican firms bill some clients in USD. QBO must be correctly configured to handle J$/USD conversion, revaluation, and forex gains/losses at year-end.

8

Partner Equity vs. PAYE

Equity partner drawings are not salary and should not attract PAYE in the same way. Incorrect classification is a common TAJ audit trigger.

9

Minimum Business Tax

All companies (including law firms) pay J$60,000 MBT annually regardless of profitability — due in two equal instalments. Easy to miss without a compliance calendar.
